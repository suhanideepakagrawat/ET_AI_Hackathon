# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two audiences the same intelligence layer serves, on two surfaces. The primary audience is Delhi residents — ordinary citizens checking what today's and tomorrow's air means for them, and especially the vulnerable: parents and schools, the elderly, people with asthma or heart conditions, outdoor workers, and pregnant women. They are usually on a phone, often outdoors, across a wide literacy and language range, and they want one answer: should I go out, and what should I do. The secondary audience is government pollution-control authorities and field inspectors, deciding under time pressure where enforcement will have the most impact. The citizen surfaces speak to residents in their own language; the dashboard and enforcement views speak to officials in the vocabulary of evidence and priority.

## Product Purpose

Turn Delhi's existing air-quality sensors into a ward-level intelligence layer that forecasts AQI 24–72 hours ahead, attributes pollution to a likely source, ranks where enforcement should go, and delivers personalized, multilingual health advice to citizens. It exists because the data already exists but the intelligence doesn't — readings without meaning, and meaning without action. Success is measured in action taken: a parent who keeps a child indoors on the right evening, an inspector sent to the ward that matters most. Not dashboards admired.

## Positioning

The one thing nothing else does: tell you which source is polluting you right now, what the air will be over the next three days, where to send inspectors, and what you personally should do about it — in your language, grounded in official health thresholds.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy civic-tech: the authority of a public-health service with the warmth of a helpful neighbor. Calm, precise, and human. It speaks plainly, shows its sources, and never overstates — guidance, not diagnosis; directional evidence, not false certainty. Confident enough that a citizen acts on it and an official can defend a decision with it.

## Anti-references

Four looks to avoid, each for a reason:

Generic AI-SaaS — cream/beige body backgrounds, a gradient hero, endless identical icon-cards, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section. Reads as machine-made and quietly erodes trust in the numbers.

Childish or toy-like — emoji as load-bearing UI, rounded-everything, bright primary-color candy. Undermines the seriousness of health and enforcement data. The current WhatsApp-styled advisory bot leans this way and should be pulled toward credible.

Boring corporate-government — navy-and-grey, stock photography, clip-art icons, walls of unstyled tables. The default portal look that citizens ignore and officials distrust.

Flashy or gimmicky — decorative animation, neon glow, glassmorphism everywhere, effects that compete with the data instead of serving it.

## Design Principles

Trust is the product. Every number traces to an authentic authority — CPCB, SAFAR, WHO, CAQM/GRAP — and every claim appears as evidence with its confidence, never asserted. If it can't be sourced, it isn't shown.

Personal, not general. The same AQI of 287 is a different message to a child, an asthmatic, an outdoor worker, and an inspector. Design for the person's context and stakes, not a one-size dashboard.

Act, don't just inform. Every surface resolves to a decision — go out or don't, mask or don't, inspect here first. Information that doesn't change a choice is noise.

Clarity across the whole spectrum. Plain language over jargon, Hindi and English as equals, voice for those who don't read fluently, and contrast that survives a bright phone screen outdoors.

Credible, not corporate. Authoritative like a health advisory, human like a neighbor — deliberately between the AI-slop and government-portal extremes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text contrast at least 4.5:1 and large text at least 3:1, verified on the tinted surfaces used for AQI bands. Multilingual by design — Hindi and English at launch, architecture ready for more Indian languages. Voice input and spoken output so low-literacy and vision-limited users get the same advice. AQI severity is never carried by color alone; it always pairs with the band name and number. Honor prefers-reduced-motion. Touch targets sized for one-handed phone use.
